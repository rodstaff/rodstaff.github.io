Rod Kay:  Introduction

When you look for someone to get a job done in web development, you would need someone who has a solid engineering background and experience.  That is, one who is flexible, a quick learner, persistent and a creative outside-of-the-box thinker; who also knows the balance between when to intensely focus independently and when to reach out collaboratively.   You would need someone with a sense of humor too.

That person would be me!

I have been coding since I was in engineering school and I recently completed a bootcamp to learn new code.   I have transitioned from hardware to software engineering.   Below is a brief summary of my story.

Circuit Developer

In the university I learned FORTRAN and even 8-bit Assembly.   After graduation, I mostly used Pascal for hardware testing/debugging when I became an analog circuit designer in the field of nanotechnology.   In addition, I also used a number of Computer Aided Design (CAD) software to simulate device performance to meet very tight device specification.   I even tweaked with the bipolar transistor models that were used in the simulation program to fit actual data measurements.   Below is the hardware design cycle we followed:

     (1) Specification 
     (2) Simulation 
     (3) Manufacturing 
     (4) Testing/Debugging: IF "fail" GOTO step (1) 
     (5) Quality Testing: IF "fail" GOTO step (1)
     (6) Production
     (7) IF "field failure" GOTO (5)

Those were days of long hours spent in front of a UNIX workstation and inside the lab but I enjoyed them immensely!

Business Developer

One day, however, I would meet a friend who would eventually convince me to do business development in the same technology industry.   It was a different kind of challenge: instead of detailed engineering reviews, I would face tough rounds of price negotiations; instead of dealing with bugs myself, I would coordinate across different departments to make sure counter-measures were quickly found so as not to impact the production schedule; delivery issues, quality issues--all sorts of commercial and customer issues!   It was a completely different world but challenging (and fun too) nevertheless.

Web Developer

In recent years, however, I have had calling--a "call back" if you will.   I have been missing those days in the "bench".   So here I am slowly but surely back in engineering since a few years ago but on the software side of things.   I even attended a bootcamp to consolidate my learning and get well acquainted with innovative, high growth companies.   I enjoy coding in NodeJS/Express and ReactJS and exploring new code that seem to come out by the week.   It is a challenging and exciting industry!

Ergo

"All work and no play?"   All the above are not really "work," they are actually my passion!   In my spare time, I also tutor math and science to both children and adults.   I love it when I help someone get into the school of their dreams or achieve the score they need on a standardized test.   I also do some cooking and intend to take it more seriously when I get a much bigger kitchen. :)
